Al-Hidayah — Shariah-Compliant Investment Framework

Alhamdulillah, Youngs Capital, a SECP-registered financial consultancy, offers its Shariah-focused framework and service line under the name Al-Hidayah (الهدايه). Al-Hidayah is built from the ground up to serve clients who seek to align their investments with Islamic principles, placing Shariah considerations at the centre of every decision rather than treating them as an afterthought.

Seeking a Halal livelihood is not only a religious obligation but also a profound act of worship. When wealth is managed with integrity and its due rights are fulfilled, it becomes a shield for one's faith and values.

Al-Hidayah is not merely a label placed upon a conventional experience. It carries its own orientation, guidelines, and customer journey — ensuring that every product, communication, and service rendered through this framework is genuinely Shariah-conscious.

Within Al-Hidayah, the following measures are consistently applied:

  • Rigorous Shariah screening is applied to all eligible securities before inclusion in any portfolio or recommendation.
  • Business activities are evaluated to exclude those involving prohibited industries such as conventional banking, alcohol, gambling, tobacco, and related sectors.
  • Financial ratios — including leverage, revenue purity, and receivables — are monitored to ensure ongoing compliance with accepted Shariah thresholds.
  • Listed securities are assessed using the following criteria: non-Shariah-compliant revenue must remain below 5%, total debt to total assets below 37%, and purification of impermissible income must be performed where applicable.
  • Structured lists, tools, and communications are maintained specifically for Shariah-conscious investors, with dedicated support separate from conventional advisory flows.

Based upon the above framework and the ongoing supervision of our appointed Shariah Advisor, we hereby certify that the Al-Hidayah service line offered by Youngs Capital is conducted in accordance with the principles of Islamic Shariah (Jurisprudence). Al-Hidayah does not eliminate investment risk, but it provides those who care about Shariah compliance a clearly defined and intentional pathway to Halal wealth management.

May Allah grant us tawfeeq to fulfil our obligations with sincerity and excellence, and may He accept these efforts in His cause. Ameen.

Al-Hidayah Shariah-Compliant Investment Service

1. Eligibility & Screening Criteria

For a company's shares to be eligible for inclusion within the Al-Hidayah framework, the following conditions must be satisfied:

  • The primary business of the company must be Halal — companies engaged in conventional banking, insurance (non-Takaful), alcohol, tobacco, gambling, or any Haram trade are excluded.
  • Non-Shariah-compliant revenue must constitute less than 5% of total annual income.
  • Total interest-bearing debt must remain below 37% of total assets.
  • Purification (Tazkiyah) of any residual impermissible income is mandatory before distribution or reinvestment.

2. Purchase & Sale of Shares — Shariah Conditions

All transactions conducted under Al-Hidayah must comply with the following Shariah conditions:

  • Valid Object (Mahalliyat): The shares must represent a Halal asset and carry a market price.
  • Ownership & Existence: The shares must exist and be in the ownership of the seller at the time of sale.
  • Possession (Qabdah): Shares must be in actual or constructive possession. In Pakistan, this is achieved through the T+1 settlement cycle.
  • Delivery Assurance: The seller must have the capacity to deliver the shares; speculative sales of non-existent assets are not permitted.
  • Absence of Gharar: All material terms — price, quantity, delivery time — must be clearly established at the point of agreement. Uncertainty rendering the contract void is impermissible.

3. Prohibited Trading Practices

The following practices are strictly prohibited under Al-Hidayah guidelines and are not available to clients of this service line:

  • Forward Sale (Bai' Salam): The sale of shares on forward-fixing or futures contracts with interest-based settlement is not permitted.
  • Short Sale & Blank Sale: Selling shares one does not own (short sale or blank sale) is impermissible, as it constitutes a sale of a non-existent asset.
  • Option Sale: Call options, put options, and compound derivatives are not available, as they involve rights that are not tradeable in Shariah without genuine ownership.
  • Leverage (Margin Trading): Leveraged trading and margin financing carry an element of Riba (interest) in the loan and are therefore excluded from this framework.
  • Repo Transactions: Sale and repurchase agreements (REPO) involve a loan with an additional benefit and are not Shariah-compliant.

4. Purification (Tazkiyah) Obligation

Where a Shariah-screened company generates any residual non-compliant income (below the 5% threshold), the proportionate amount must be purified by the investor via charitable giving (Sadaqah) without expectation of reward. Youngs Capital — Al-Hidayah will notify clients of the applicable purification ratio periodically.

5. General Disclaimer & Risk Disclosure

Shariah compliance does not eliminate investment risk. Markets are subject to price fluctuation, and past performance is not indicative of future results. Al-Hidayah provides a Shariah-structured framework; it does not guarantee returns. All investment decisions remain the responsibility of the investor.
